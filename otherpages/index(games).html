C@RRION Description

So some traits of games have started becoming more and more popular. These traits include permadeath, dungeon crawling, and random generation (my personal favorite). These are known as "rogue-like" elements and the games who use most of them are either known as "rogue-lites" or "rogue-likes" as well (the line between the two are being blurred). The origin of this name goes back to a older text based game simply known as rogue.


While I haven't played many of the pioneers of this genre I love these types of games. My favorite game is a roguelike and the replayability of the near infinite amount of dungeons will keep it there for a long time.



To get to the point, I decided to try my hand at making a rogue-like. With my practice (which may be too little) I have started developing the game C@RRION. @ for the original model of a character and "carrion" for rotting flesh. The plot is going to be about and undead knight going through a dungeon to reclaim his humanity.

Ping


This is a small little completely original game that is like table tennis. Completely original. It's one player only right now but expect that to change.


LSPaint


This is a small painting application that I made to test out custom buttons. You can click the icons on the left side of the screen to change most aspects of the brush and background (middle click to turn on eraser). There are 4 brush types, some of which require a stroke but unfortantly due to not being able to set the framerate very high most leave spots if you don't move the mouse slowly, but it works. Other than that there isn't much else.


Bkgrnd


A custom background generator. I actually use this for my desktop. CONTROLS Q- Rectangle on/off W- Circle on/off E- Triangle on/off R- Line on/off T- Dots on/off A- Back Layer on/off S- Front Layer on/off D- Stop on/off Z- Random Colors on X- Monochrome on
